Unit 1: Prepare for work

Definitions of OHS requirements

OHS requirements are legislation or regulations codes of practice and enterprise safety policies and procedures. This may include protective clothing and equipment, use of tooling and equipment, workplace environment and safety, handling of material, use of fire-fighting equipment, enterprise first aid, hazard control and hazardous materials and substances. The goals of occupational safety and health(OHS) is fostering a safe and healthy work environment and reduce hazard.

Types of hazards

  • Biological bacteria, viruses, insects, plants, birds, animals and humans, etc...
  • Chemical depends on the physical, chemical and toxic properties.
  • Ergonomic repetitive movements, improper set up of workstation, poor design of equipment, workstation design, (postural) or workflow, manual handling. etc...
  • Physical slippery floors, objects in walkways, unsafe or misused machinery, excessive noise, poor lighting, fire. radiation, magnetic fields, pressure extremes (high pressure or vacuum), noise, etc....
  • Psychological Shift work, workload, dealing with the public, harassment, discrimination, threat of danger, constant low-level noise and stress. Stress, violence, etc...
  • Safety slipping or tripping hazards, inappropriate machine guarding, equipment malfunctions or breakdowns.

Concept of safety

Safety is a state in which hazards and conditions leading to physical, psychological or material harm are controlled in order to preserve the health and well-being of individuals and the community.

Safety equipment and tools

  • Dust masks
  • Safety goggle
  • Glove
  • Work wear
  • First aid and safety shoe and etc...

Work instruction is a description of the specific tasks and activities within an organization.

A Job can be defined as:-

  • The execution or performance of a task.
  • Anything a person is expected to do, duty, responsibility.
  • A piece of work especially a specific done as part of the routine of one's occupation or for an agreed price.
  • A post of employment; full-time or part-time position and etc...

Job requirments

  • Discipline
  • Enthusiasm an inner love for the job.
  • Qualifications this is a more material, tactile need for a job which can be conveyed through degrees and certificates.
  • Soft skills include those skills which ensure that a job is executed well and the employee can carry himself in the proper manner too. For example good and smooth communication, computer skills, proficiency in language.

Procedures vs Work instructions

Procedures describe a process, while a work instruction describes how to perform the conversion itself.
Questions that need to be answered in a procedure include:-

  • Where do the inputs come from (suppliers)?
  • Where do the outputs go (customers)?
  • Who performs what action when (responsibilities)?
  • How do you know when you have done it right (effectiveness criteria)?
  • What feedback should be captured (metrics)?
  • How do we communicate results (charts, graphs and reports)?
  • What laws (regulations) or standards apply?

Job specification

A statement of workers characteristics and qualifications required for satisfactory performance of defined duties and tasks comprising a specific job or function.

Unit 2: MUDA and its problem

Definition of MUDA

MUDA is a Japanese word meaning wasteful activity.

The 3M

Muda, mura and muri all are japanese word.

  • Muda means wasteful. Muda refers to activities that don't add value.
  • Mura means unevenness.
  • Muri means overburden.

Seven deadly of wastes/Muda

  • Overproduction To produce things more than necessary.It is called the worst kind of Muda.
  • Inventory the situation where items such as raw materials, work in process and finished goods which are not having value added to them.
  • Motion unnecessary movements
  • Conveyance or Transportation It is unnecessary transportation.
  • Waiting or Idle time refers to both human and machine waiting.
  • Defect making this includes defects, inspections for defects in-process and claims, rescheduling and resource loss.
  • Processing this consists of processing and operations primarily unnecessary. It is processing beyond the standard required by the customer.

Concept of planning

Planning is the most basic and primary function of management. It is the pre decided outline of the activities to be conducted in the organization. Planning is the process of deciding when, what, when where and how to do a certain activity before starting to work.

What is kaizen?

Kaizen a Japanese business philosophy of continuous improvement of working practices.

Kaizen visual management boards

Kaizen visual management boards are key visual communication tools that help teams and organizations work harder to manage their continuous improvement efforts. Kaizen visual management boards benefits:-

  • Improve productivity
  • Impress clients
  • Reduce waste
  • Raise awareness and etc...

Definitions of work place

A workplace is a location where someone works for their employer or a place of employment.

Concept of plant layout

Plant layout is a plan for effective utilization of facilities for the manufacture of products.
Types of plant layout:- Two basic plans of the arrangement of manufacturing facilities are:- product layout and process layout.

  • Product layout(Line layout) in this type of layout, all the machines are arranged in the sequence, as required to produce a specific product. It is called line layout because machines are arrange in a straight line.
  • Process layout(Functional layout) in this type of layout, all machines performing similar type of operations are grouped at one location.
  • Combination layout in practice, plants are rarely laid out either in product or process layout form.
  • Fixed it is also called stationary layout.n this type of layout men, materials and machines are brought to a product that remains in one place owing to its size.

Process Flow Diagram(PFD)

A Process Flow Diagram(PFD) is a type of flowchart that illustrates the relationships between major components at an industrial plant.

A typical Process Flow Diagram(PFD) for a single unit process will include these elements:-

  • Major equipment: including names and ID numbers. Examples include compressors, mixers, vessels, pumps, boilers and coolers.
  • Process piping: moves the product, usually fluids, between equipment pieces.
  • Operational data: such as pressure, temperature, density, mass flow rate and massenergy balance. Values often will include minimum, normal and maximum.
  • Process flow direction
  • Control valves and process-critical valves
  • Major bypass and recirculation systems
  • Composition of fluids
  • Process stream names
  • Connections with other systems

To identify waste or Muda the following tools and equipment are used:-

  • Tape or meter is used to measure distances or lengths.
  • Stop watch is used to measure processing or waiting times.
  • Photo camera may be necessary to take pictures, such as shop layout, for analysis.
  • Video camera may be necessary to record video of each work element to study and identify wastes, such as motion, processing, waiting, etc...
  • Calculator required to make arithmetic calculations.

Causes and effects of MUDA

Causes of Overproduction

  • Large-lot production
  • Anticipatory production
  • Inability to achieve short changeover times with the large equipment used in mass production systems
  • Creating enough stock to replace the number of defective parts produced
  • Overstaffing or too much equipment
  • Machines that turn out parts too quickly

Effects of Overproduction

  • Anticipatory buying of parts and materials
  • Blocked flow of goods
  • Increased inventory
  • No flexibility in planning
  • Occurrence of defects

Causes of Inventory

  • Acceptance of inventory as normal
  • Poor equipment layout
  • Long changeover times
  • Large lot production
  • Obstructed flow of goods
  • Anticipatory production
  • Defective parts
  • Upstream process is too fast for the downstream process

Effects Inventory

  • Waste of space
  • Needs for inspection and transportation
  • Expansion of working fund
  • Shelf life may expire
  • It ties up cash
  • Makes FIFO inventory management more difficult

Causes of motion

  • Isolated operations
  • Low employee morale
  • Poor work layout
  • Lack of training
  • Undeveloped skill

Effects of motion

  • Increase in manpower and processing
  • Unstable operation
  • Increases production time
  • Can cause injury

Causes of Conveyance or Transportation

  • Poor layout
  • Shish-skilled workers
  • Sitting to perform operations
  • The need for conveyance systems is assumed

Effects of Conveyance or Transportation

  • Waste of space
  • Production deterioration
  • Expansion of transportation facilities
  • Occurrence of scratches
  • Increase production time and cost
  • Wastes time and energy

Causes of Waiting or Idle time

  • Obstruction of flow
  • Poor equipment layout
  • Trouble at the upstream process
  • Capacity imbalances
  • Large Lot-production

Effects of Waiting or Idle time

  • Waste of manpower, time, & machines
  • Increase in the in-process inventory
  • Failed delivery dates
  • Poor workflow continuity

Causes of Defect making

  • Emphasis on downstream inspection
  • No standard for inspection work
  • Omission of standard operations
  • Material handling and conveyance

Effects of Defect making

  • Increase in material cost
  • Productivity deterioration
  • Increase in personnel & processes for inspection
  • Increase in defects and claims
  • Invite reworking costs

Causes of Processing

  • Inadequate study of processes
  • Inadequate study of operations
  • Incomplete standardization
  • Materials are not studied

Effects of Processing

  • Unnecessary processes or operation
  • Increase in manpower and man-hour
  • Lower workability
  • Increase in defects
  • Can reduce life of components

Total Productive Maintenance(TPM)

TPM brings maintenance into focus as a necessary and vitally important part of the business.

Important of Total Productive Maintenance(TPM)

  • Avoid wastage in a quickly changing economic environment
  • Producing goods without reducing product quality
  • Reduce waste cost
  • Produce a low batch quantity at the earliest possible time
  • Goods send to the customers must be non-defective

Total Productive Maintenance(TPM) targets

  • Productivity
  • Cost reduction
  • Quality
  • Delivery time
  • Safety
  • Moral
  • Obtain Minimum 80% Overall Plant Efficiency
  • Obtain Minimum 90% Overall Equipment Effectiveness
  • Run the machines even during lunch

Total Productive Maintenance(TPM) objectives

  • Achieve Zero Breakdown and Zero accidents in all functional areas of the organization.
  • Involve people in all levels of organization
  • Form different teams to reduce defects and self maintenance

Direct benefits of Total Productive Maintenance(TPM)

  • Increase productivity
  • Rectify customer complaints
  • Reduce the manufacturing cost by 30%
  • Satisfy the customers needs
  • Reduce accidents
  • Follow pollution control measure and etc...

Indirect benefits of Total Productive Maintenance(TPM)

  • Higher confidence level among the employees
  • Keep the work place clean and attractive
  • Favorable change in the attitude of the operators
  • Achieve goals by working as team
  • Share knowledge and experience
  • The workers get a feeling of owning the machine and etc...

Factors affecting equipment effectiveness

  • Equipment failure(breakdown)
  • Setup and adjustment downtime
  • Idling and minor stoppages
  • Reduced speed
  • Process defects
  • Reduced yield

Types of maintenance

  • Breakdown maintenance repair is done after the equipment failure or stoppage. This concept has disadvantage unplanned stoppages, excessive damage, spare parts problems, high repair costs, excessive waiting and maintenance time and high trouble shooting problems.
  • Preventive maintenance activities that are undertaken after a specified period of time or amount of machine use.
  • Periodic maintenance(Time based maintenance) time based maintenance consists of periodically inspecting, servicing and cleaning equipment and replacing parts to prevent sudden failure and process problems.
  • Predictive maintenance this is a method in which the service life of important part is predicted based on inspection or diagnosis, in order to use the parts to the limit of their service life.
  • Maintenance prevention it indicates the design of a new equipment.
  • Corrective maintenance prevent equipment failures is further expanded to be applied to the improvement.

Benefits of addressing waste

  • Conserve resources
  • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions
  • Streamline reporting and information sharing and etc...
