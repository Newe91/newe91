Unit 1: Clean equipment

Cleaning tools

  • Cloth
  • Water or rubbing alcohol
  • Portable vacuum
  • Cotton swabs
  • Foam swabs
  • Air compressor and etc...

Why cleaning needed to computer hardware?

Makes the entire system run more smoothly and keep it running longer.

What is the importance of maintaining hardware tools?

Makes any home improvement or repair project easier, safer and more successful. Proper tool care also saves you money.

Precautions

Precautions are guidelines to be followed to prevent damage to equipment or injury to people.
The following are very important precautions:-

  • Before cleaning any electrical equipment make sure that it is switched off and unplugged from the mains.
  • Allow certain equipment such as monitors and laser printers to cool down and lose their capacitance before cleaning them.
  • When cleaning inside the PC or handling parts from a PC such as hard disk or circuit boards you should earth yourself using antistatic wrist strap.
  • Always refer to the manufactures manual before attempting to clean any equipment, because improper cleaning or maintenance may be dangerous and also may invalidate your warranty.
  • Some equipment such as power supplies and monitors use voltages and therefore should only be maintained by specially trained people.

Maintenance documentation

Maintenance documentation is any record containing information that you might need to complete maintenance tasks and inspections.

Recommended maintenance schedule

  • Daily Virus scan and spyware scan.
  • Weekly Data backups,antivirus software updates,windows updates other software updates,disk cleanup deleting temporary browser files, history and cookies.
  • Monthly Disk defragment .

Unit 2: Replace and maintain consumables and supplies

Consumables

Consumables are goods that require recurrent replacement because they are used up or transformed in their use. Consumables must be replaced regularly.

Example consumable materials

  • Paper, pens, scissors, file folders
  • Calculators
  • Whiteboard markers
  • Printer paper and etc...

Capital asset

A capital asset is more expensive than a consumable item and isn't something you would buy every day in your business.

Examples of capital asset

  • Computer
  • Printer
  • Scanner and etc...

Dispose consumables

Dispose of used consumables such as waste ink, pre-treatment solution, cleaning solution and the ink charging unit.

Unit 3: Maintain equipment

Definition of equipment

Equipment is any tangible property that is used in the operations of a business.

List of equipments

  • Printer
  • Monitor
  • Switches and hubs
  • Modem and router
  • Optical drives
  • Motherboard
  • Mouse
  • Keyboard and etc...

What is Personal Protective Equipment(PPE)?

Personal protective equipment (PPE) refers to protective clothing, helmets, goggles, or other garments or equipment designed to protect the wearer's body from injury. The purpose of personal protective equipment is to reduce employee exposure to hazards.

List of personal protective equipments

  • Goggles and face protection it use for eye and face protection
  • Safety shoes it use for foot protection
  • Gloves hand protection

Hazards

  • Falling objects
  • Objects that could puncture the skin
  • Objects that could roll over workers feet
  • Toxic chemicals
  • Heat
  • Harmful dust
  • Radiation and etc...

Uninterruptible Power Supply(UPS)

The UPS is designed to protect your computer from sudden lapses in power, power spikes or surges current. A UPS is essentially a battery that is designed to take over when there is a power loss.

Electrostatic discharge(ESD)

ESD occurs when two charged objects come into contact with each other and energy is transferred between them.

List of high-voltage equipment

  • Power Supply
  • Monitor
  • Lasers: employ a high-intensity light beam that can cause severe damage to your eyes including blindness.
