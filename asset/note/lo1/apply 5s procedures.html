Prepare for work

Work instructions

Is a description of the specific tasks and activities within an organization

Procedures vs Work Instructions

Procedures describe a process, while Work Instructions describes how to perform the conversion itself.
PDCA process approach (Plan, Do, Check, Act) is used to capture the relevant information.

Questions that need to be answered in a procedure include:-

  • Where do the inputs come from (suppliers)?
  • Where do the outputs go (customers)?
  • Who performs what action when (responsibilities)?
  • How do you know when you have done it right (effectiveness criteria)?
  • What feedback should be captured (metrics)?
  • How do we communicate results (charts, graphs and reports)?

Job

  • The execustion of a task
  • A piece of work especially a specific done as part of the routine of one's occupation or for an agreed price.
  • Anything aperson expected to do,duty, responsibilt.

Job Specification

A statement of workers qualifications required for satisfactory performance of defined tasks comprising a specific job.

Job requirments

  • Discipline
  • Enthusiasm
  • Qualifications
  • Soft skills

OHS(Occupational safety and health) requirement

OHS requirement are legislation codes of practice and enterprise safety policies and procedures. This may include protective clothing and equipment, use of tooling and equipment, workplace environment and safety, handling of material, use of firefighting equipment, enterprise first aid, hazard control and hazardous materials and substances. Occupational safety and health(OSH) also commonly focus on workplace health and safety (WHS).

The goal of OHS

The goals of occupational safety and health programs include fostering a safe and healthy work environment and also reduce hazard.

Hazard means anything that could cause harm.

List of Workplace hazards
  • Physical factors
  • Chemicals
  • Biological
  • ergonomic conditions
  • Allergens
  • Psychosocial risk factors

What is 5S?

5S is a systematized approach to organize work environment. 5S(Sort,Set in order,Shine,Standardize,Sustain) activities are to create good working environment.

    What is the benefits of 5S?
  • Improves creativity people
  • Improves communicaton among people
  • Improves teamwork among people and etc...
    What are the visible results(benefits) of 5S?
  • Proper storage
  • Improved productivity
  • Decreases delay
  • Zero breakdown
  • Early detection of problems
  • Decrease in the number of accidents
  • Low employee turnover and etc...
    What are the Invisible results(benefits) of 5S?
  • Happier employees with high morale
  • Happier customers

Definition of Sort

Focuses on eliminating unnecessary items from the workplace.
Categorize equipment, furniture, tool in your working place into the following 3 categories:-

  • Necessary
  • Unnecessary
  • May not necessary

Benefits of sort activity

  • Work environment have more space
  • Save time
  • Improves work flow
  • Increase productivity
  • Communication between workers is improved
  • Product quality is increased
  • Sorting can lead to a much safer workplace;By clearing out the items you no longer need

If the first pillar(sort) is not well implemented, the following types of problems occur:

  • A workshop becomes increasingly crowded and hard to work in
  • Time is wasted in searching for parts and tools
  • Increase unnecessary maintenance cost of unneeded inventory and machinery
  • Unneeded items make it harder to improve the process flow
  • Excess stock-on-hand hides other types of problems in production
  • Unnecessary lockers, shelves, cabinets and items make communication between employees difficult

Listing Necessary and unnecessary items

Some of types of unnecessary items are:-

  • Outdated or broken tools
  • Worn-out bits
  • Defective or excess quantities of small parts and inventory
  • Electrical equipment with broken cords
  • Outdated posters, signs, notices and memos
  • Old rags and other cleaning supplies

Use Red tag strategy

A Red tag is a red colored tag used to identify items no longer needed in a particular work area.Place “Red tag” for categorization of items to identify unnecessary items.

    An item with a red tag is asking three questions:-
  • Is this item needed?
  • If it is needed, is it needed in this quantity?
  • If it is needed, does it need to be located here?

Places of where unnecessary items accumulate

Some locations where unneeded items tend to accumulate are:-

  • In corners next to exists
  • Under the eaves of warehouses
  • Near the bottom of tall stacks of items
  • Areas not designated for any particular purpose
  • In tools boxes that are not clearly sorted

Definition of Set in order

Set in order means arranging necessary items.

Benefits of set in order

  • Inventory control
  • The visual workplace and employee morale
  • Motion wastes
  • Searching time wastes
  • The waste of human energy
  • The waste of excess inventory
  • The waste of defective products

The principles that are helpful to eliminate or reduce motions that operators make are:

  • Principle 1: Start and end each motion with both hands moving at once.
  • Principle 2: Both arms should move symmetrically and in opposite directions.
  • Principle 3: Keep trunk motions to a minimum.
  • Principle 4: Use gravity instead of muscle.
  • Principle 5: Avoid zigzagging motions and sudden changes in direction.
  • Principle 6: Move with a steady rhythm.
  • Principle 7: Maintain a comfortable posture with comfortable motions.
  • Principle 8: Use the feet to operate on and off switches for machines where practical.
  • Principle 9: Keep materials and tools close and in front.
  • Principle 10: Arrange materials and tools in the order of their use.
  • Principle 11: Use inexpensive methods for feeding in and sending out materials.
  • Principle 12: Stand at a proper height for the work to be done.
  • Principle 13: Make materials and parts easy to pick up.
  • Principle 14: Make handles and grips in efficient, easy-to-use shapes and positions.

Motion Economy strategy

We can remove motion waste from existing operation. By using human body appropriately, by organizing the workplace and by redesigning of tools and equipment’s, we can minimize motion waste

Visual control Strategy

A visual control is any communication device used in the work environment that tells us at a glance how work should be done.

Signboard strategy

uses signboards to identify what, where and how many. The three main types of signboards are:

  • Location indicators that show where items go
  • Item indicators that show what specific items go in those places
  • Amount indicators that show how many of these items belong there

Definition of Shine

Shine means cleaning up workplace daily.
Sweeping floors, wiping off machinery and generally making sure that everything in the factory stays clean

Benefits of shine

  • Turn the workplace in to clean
  • Important for employees physical and mental health
  • Work efficiency and safety and etc...

If the shine is not well implemented, the following types of problems occur:

  • Poor morale and inefficiency at work.
  • Unable to see or find defects in dark and messy workplaces
  • Slipping and injuries can be created due to puddles of oil and water on the floor.
  • Low morale due to filthy work environments.
  • Shaving cuts can get in to people’s eyes and create injuries.
  • Low and unsafe operating machines due to insufficient checkups and maintenance which in turn leads to hazard and accidents and etc...

Tools and materials used to implement shine

  • Sponge
  • Broom
  • Brush spade
  • Vacuum cleaner bolts
  • Garbage containers
  • Screws etc…

Definition of Standardize

Maintain an environment where 3s(sort,set in order,shine) are implemented in the same manner throughout the organization. To increasing efficiency by reducing waste.

Definition of Sustain

Maintain S1-S4(sort,set in order,shine,standardize) through discipline, commitment and empowerment. All about make them as culture.
