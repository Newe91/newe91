Unit of Competence: Providing first level remote help desk support

Theory

What is a help desk?

  • Help desk is an organization that provides a single point of contact for users in need of technical support.
  • A help desk is a resource designed to provide end users with information and assistance regarding problems with computers and related devices or software.
  • A help desk is an information and assistance resource that troubleshoots problems with computers or similar products.
  • A help Desk is a resource designed for IT users to contact when they are having problems with their IT services.
  • Goal of help desk such as:- To enhance client satisfaction by effectively and efficiently resolving problems and questions

Function of help-desk

  • It provides a single point of contact for users to receive help on computer issues
  • It manages its requests via help desk software

Best practices help desk must include:-

  • Single point of contact(SPOC) for IT interruptions
  • Computer or software consultations
  • Tracking capabilities of all incoming problems
  • Problem escalation procedures
  • Problem resolution

Log book

Log book is a simple method of keeping track of the manufacturing process.

The five important things that should be included in a help desk support logbook:-

  • Problem
  • Solution
  • Who see the problem
  • Time and date
  • Source of the problem

Steps in the incident management process

  • Receive the incident

    Sources of incidents:-

    • In person
    • Phone call
    • E-mail message
    • Web based contact
    Goals of receive the incident:-
    • Establish a relationship with end user
    • Get basic information from user
    • May use a specific greeting script
    • Warn that the call may be monitored
    • Apologize for any delay or wait time

  • Prescreen the incident

    Prescreening is a filtering process to determine how the help desk staff will handle the incident.
    Goal of prescreen the incident:-

    • Product information
    • How to order
    • Where to purchase

  • Authenticate the user

    Authentication determines whether help desk staff are authorized to handle an incident.
    Usually involves checking:-

    • Product registration database
    • Product model or serial number
    • Warranty database
    • Support service database of authorized clients
    Goal of authenticate the user:-
    • Determine the status of each client
    • Establish a billing procedure(where appropriate)
    • Filter out unauthorized clients

  • Log the incident

    Incident logging begins to document the incident and its related problem. Basic information about the incident is recorded:-

    • Trouble report form
    • Incident tracking database
    Goal of log the incident:-
    • Start a record of the incident

  • Screen the incident

    Common incident categories:-

    • Request for information(that could not be handled during prescreening)
    • Question
    • Problem
    • Complaint
    • Work order
    Goal of screen the incident:-
    • Define the category of an incident
    • Capture a brief description of the incident

  • Prioritize the incident

    Priority code is assigned based on:-

    • How serious the problem is for users
    • How many users are affected
    • Consequence of not handling problem immediately
    Goal of prioritize the incident:-
    • A priority code often determines the kind of attention an incident will receive from staff
    • Handle incidents on first-in, first-out (FIFO) basis
    Example priority codes:-
    • Urgent
    • High priority
    • Medium priority
    • Low priority

  • Assign the incident

    When a level 1 Incident Screener cannot respond to an incident directly, they assign it to another agent who Has specific product knowledge and Has specialized expertise.
    Goal of assign the incident:-

    • Move an incident to a queue where it will get appropriate attention.

  • Track the incident

    Goal of track the incident:-

    • To reduce mistake
    • To see al update

  • Escalate the incident

    Escalate means transfer to problem to higher level supporter if the help desk not resolve the problem.
    Goal of escalate the incident:-

    • To resolve big problem

  • Resolve the incident

    Resolution means that a user's problem has been solved or information has been provided.
    Goal of resolve the incident:-

    • Minimize the percent of incidents that cannot be resolved satisfactorily
    Caveats:-
    • Not all calls can be completely resolved by the Incident Management Process.
    • Resolution doesn't necessarily mean the client is completely satisfied.

  • Close the incident

    Incident closing may include:-

    • Review the steps to solve the problem
    • Seek mutual agreement(verification) that a solution has been reached
    • Thank the user for contacting the help desk
    • Invite the user to recontact if not satisfied
    • Make final entries in incident log or database
    Goal of incident closing:-
    • Provide technical and interpersonal closure to the incident

  • Archive the incident

    Archive means to copy resolved incidents to a database of completed incidents Incidents are retained in an active database as long as they remain relevant Goal of archive the incident:-

    • Reduce the size of the active database by removing less relevant information
    The archives:-
    • Can be searched if needed in future problem-solving situations
    • Can serve as a source of data for statistical analysis

Physical layout of help desk work areas

  • Desk in a cubicle
  • Access to one or more computer systems
  • Access to reference library
  • Telephone headset permits freedom of motion

Hardware and software tools are used to manage incidents

  • Help desk software packages
  • Computer telephony systems
    • Automated call distributor(ACD)
    • Interactive voice response(IVR)
  • Web-based support

Help desk software features

  • Log and track incidents

    • Manage incident queues
    • Interface with telephone system
    • Set incident priorities
    • Assign incidents to support staff
    • Escalate incidents

  • Contact information

    Store, edit and recall contact and location information in a contact database

  • Product information

    • Product features
    • Product limitations
    • New versions
    • Configuration constraints
    • Known bugs
    • Product availability

  • Configuration information for client systems

    • Hardware configuration
    • Software licenses
    • Network protocols

  • Diagnostic utilities

    Diagnostic software is used to analyze performance of a remote system and look for potential problem areas

  • Problem solutions knowledge base

    Contains information about common problems and their solutions Sometimes called a “smart” database.
    May use search strategies based on artificial intelligence such as:-

    • Expert systems(sequences of IF-THEN rules)
    • Neural networks(automated learning systems)
    • Case-based reasoning(pattern-matching strategies)

  • Product order entry

    • Order entry capability
    • Can integrate with other business systems, such as shipping and invoicing

  • Client feedback

    Client feedback measures the level of satisfaction with Products and services,Help desk support.

  • Asset management

    • Equipment inventory
    • System installation information

  • Service management

    • Warranty information
    • Reminders of next preventive maintenance
    • Service history

  • Telephone system interface

    Manages large number of incoming and outgoing calls Links to communication and information resource:-

    • E-mail
    • Internet
    • Online help
    • Product documentation
    • Problem archives

  • Statistical reports

    Predefined reports

    • Abandonment rate(callers who hang up)
    • Number of unresolved incidents
    • Average length of time on hold
    • Average time to resolve problems
    • Productivity of agents
    • Inventory control reports
    • Frequently asked questions

Computer telephony systems

Computer telephony is the integration of computer and telephone technology into a seamless help desk tool.
Automated Call Distributor(ACD) is a computer telephony system that automates the first steps in incident management (answer calls ,greet callers,provide menus and route the call to support agents).
Goal of computer telephony systems:-

  • Reduce amount of time and cost to respond to calls and route them to support agents
  • Collect information about performance of help desk operation
  • Monitor calls
Problem in computer telephony systems:-
  • Reputation for poor customer service
  • Poor design of menus
  • Lengthy hold times
  • Repetitious requests for information
  • Dropped calls

  • Knowledge bases

E-mail support advantages

E-mail is asynchronous user and support staff do not have to be available online at exactly the same time. E-mail responses make more flexible use of support agent's time. E-mail responses to frequent questions can be composed in advance and then pasted into messages

Impact of web and e-mail use on support staff

  • Need more efficient use of support staff resources
  • Writing skills become more important for support staff than telephone skills
  • Quick recall is less important than ability to locate information
  • Ability to listen is less important than ability to read and understand
  • Customer service skills remain important

Trends in help desk operations

  • Increased use of voice recognition and artificial intelligence in support products.
  • Help desk operations will become more proactive(anticipate user problems) and less reactive
  • Help desks will play significant role in client relationship management(CRM)
Client relationship management(CRM) is a business process that aims to meet the needs of clients by providing excellent client service.
