Unit 1: Identify and analyze maintenance needs

Determine maintenance strategy

Determine maintenance strategy involves selecting an approach to ensure that equipment facilities and system remain in good manner. Maintenance is the act of maintaining something such as keeping equipment in good condition by making repairs and correcting problems etc...

The choice of maintenance strategy depends on factor such as:-

  • Types of asset
  • Available resources
  • Organization group

Identify and analyze maintenance need is a crucial step in developing and effective maintenance strategy.

Key step to identify and analyze maintenance need:-

  • Asset inventory
  • Criteria assessment
  • Condition monitoring
  • Failure mode and effect analysis(FMEA)
  • Maintain history analysis
  • Resource assessment
  • Life cycle cost analysis
  • Operational and environmental analysis
  • Stakeholder Input

Some risk of business based maintenance strategy:-

  • Reactive maintenance

    • Risk: Reliance on reactive maintenance may lead to unplanned downtime and disruptions if equipment fails unexpectedly.
    • Impact: Production delays, increased repair costs, potential damage to other interconnected systems.

  • Preventive Maintenance

    • Risk: Strict reliance on scheduled preventive maintenance without considering actual equipment condition may result in unnecessary downtime and costs.
    • Impact: Unplanned downtime during scheduled maintenance, potential over-maintenance leading to increased costs.

  • Predictive Maintenance

    • Risk: Relying solely on predictive maintenance without a backup plan may result in missed predictions or false alarms.
    • Impact: Unplanned failures if the predictive system fails to accurately predict issues, potential under-maintenance if false positives are common.

  • Proactive Maintenance(RCM)

    • Risk: Inadequate analysis of failure modes and consequences in RCM may lead to incorrect prioritization of maintenance tasks.
    • Impact: Unplanned failures of critical assets, potential escalation of issues due to misjudged criticality.

  • Condition-Based Maintenance

    • Risk: Inaccurate or insufficient monitoring may lead to missed signs of equipment deterioration.
    • Impact: Unplanned downtime, potential for critical failures if condition-based triggers are not reliable.

  • Total Productive Maintenance(TPM)

    • Risk: Overemphasis on equipment efficiency may lead to neglect of other critical maintenance needs.
    • Impact: Unaddressed issues that may lead to failures, reduced overall equipment effectiveness.

  • Resource Constraints

    • Risk: Inadequate resources allocated to maintenance, regardless of strategy, may result in delayed or insufficient maintenance activities.
    • Impact: Increased risk of equipment failures, higher costs associated with emergency repairs.

Reviewing systems architecture and configuration documentation

Reviewing systems architecture and configuration documentation is a critical step in developing and maintaining an effective maintenance strategy. This process ensures that the maintenance strategy aligns with the characteristics and requirements of the systems in place.

Here is how you can approach the review:

  • Understand Systems Architecture:

    Objective: Gain a comprehensive understanding of the overall systems architecture, including hardware, software, network components and their interdependencies.
    Considerations: Identify critical components, redundancies and any specific requirements or limitations.

  • Assess Configuration Documentation:

    Objective: Evaluate the documentation that outlines the configuration settings and parameters of the systems.
    Considerations: Ensure that configuration details are accurate, up-to-date and aligned with industry best practices and security standards

  • Identify Critical Components:

    Objective: Identify components crucial to system functionality and business operations.
    Considerations: Assess the impact of failures or performance degradation in critical components on overall system performance and business continuity.

  • Review Maintenance History:

    Objective: Analyze historical maintenance records to understand past issues and resolutions.
    Considerations: Look for patterns or recurring issues that may indicate the need for adjustments to the maintenance strategy or changes in system configuration.

  • Check Compliance with Standards:

    Objective: Ensure that the systems and configurations comply with relevant industry standards, regulatory requirements and security protocols.
    Considerations: Identify any gaps in compliance and address them to minimize risks.

  • Evaluate System Performance Monitoring:

    Objective: Review the system performance monitoring capabilities.
    Considerations: Ensure that monitoring tools are in place to track key performance indicators, identify anomalies and provide early warnings of potential issues.

  • Assess Scalability and Flexibility:

    Objective: Evaluate how well the systems can scale to accommodate growth or changes in demand.
    Considerations: Ensure that the maintenance strategy accounts for scalability requirements and can adapt to changes in system configuration.

  • Check Backup and Recovery Configurations:

    Objective: Review backup and recovery configurations to ensure they align with business continuity objectives.
    Considerations: Verify the frequency of backups, the integrity of data restoration processes and the recovery time objectives.

  • Evaluate Security Configurations:

    Objective: Assess the security configurations of the systems.
    Considerations: Verify that security measures are in place and aligned with the organization's security policies. Consider any potential vulnerabilities that may impact the maintenance strategy.

  • Review Documentation Accessibility:

    Objective: Confirm that documentation is easily accessible to maintenance personnel.
    Considerations: Ensure that relevant teams can quickly retrieve necessary information for troubleshooting, maintenance planning and system modifications.

Identifying risks and problems

Identifying risks and potential problems is a crucial step in developing a robust maintenance strategy. By recognizing potential challenges, organizations can proactively plan and implement measures to mitigate these risks.

Here is a comprehensive guide to identifying risks and problems for a maintenance strategy:-

  • Asset criticality and failure modes

    Risk: Failure of critical assets can significantly impact operations.
    Identify: Assess the criticality of each asset and identify potential failure modes and their consequences.

  • Preventive maintenance schedule

    Risk: Over-reliance on preventive maintenance can lead to unnecessary costs.
    Identify: Evaluate the preventive maintenance schedule to ensure it aligns with equipment needs and industry best practices.

  • Reactive maintenance

    Risk: Relying solely on reactive maintenance can lead to unplanned downtime.
    Identify: Evaluate the frequency and impact of unplanned breakdowns.

  • Predictive maintenance accuracy

    Risk:Inaccurate predictions from predictive maintenance tools can lead to unnecessary interventions.
    Identify: Assess the accuracy and reliability of predictive maintenance data and systems.

  • Equipment age and obsolescence

    Risk: Can lead to increased failure rates.
    Identify: Evaluate the age and condition of critical equipment. Plan for equipment upgrades or replacements.

  • Supply chain disruptions

    Risk: Delays or disruptions in the supply chain can impact the availability of spare parts.
    Identify: Assess the resilience of the supply chain and identify alternative sources for critical components.

  • Human factors

    Risk: Lack of skilled personnel or human error can impact maintenance effectiveness.
    Identify: Evaluate the skills of maintenance personnel and provide training as needed. Implement procedures to minimize human error.

Unit 2: Develop service level agreements

Service level agreement

Service level agreement is an agreement of suppler and clients about product or service.
Service Level Agreement(SLA) is a formal document that outlines the terms, expectations and standards for the services provided by one party to another. In the context of maintenance strategy, SLAs play a crucial role in establishing clear guidelines and expectations between the maintenance provider (internal maintenance team or external service provider) and the stakeholders (such as operations, management or clients).

The purpose of service level agreement(SLA):-

  • Define service scope
  • Established maintenance metrics
  • Clarifying responsibility
  • Ensure accountability
  • Manage expectations

Key components of maintenance service level agreement(SLA):-

  • Service description
  • Performance metrics
  • Responsibility and role
  • Response and resolution time
  • Contract duration and renewal terms
  • Penalize and incentives
  • Communication protocols
  • Performance reviews

Benefit of maintenance service level agreement(SLA):-

  • Continue improvement
  • Clarify and alignment
  • Performance monitoring
  • Accountability
  • Establish a clear understanding of maintenance expectations and align service delivery with organizational goals

Unit 3: Formulate maintenance strategy

Identify and Examine maintenance options and strategy

Identifying and examining maintenance options and strategies involve a thorough analysis of various approaches to managing assets, equipment and facilities.

Preventive maintenance

Scheduled maintenance activities performed to prevent equipment failure.
Advantages of preventive maintenance:-

  • Reduces the likelihood of unexpected breakdowns.
  • Extends the lifespan of equipment.
Considerations
Requires regular scheduling and adherence to maintenance plans.

Predictive maintenance

Monitors equipment conditions in real-time to predict when maintenance is needed.
Advantages of predictive maintenance:-

  • Minimizes downtime by addressing issues just before failure.
  • Optimizes maintenance efforts based on actual equipment conditions.
Considerations
Requires advanced monitoring technology and data analytics.

Corrective maintenance

Addresses issues as they arise, typically in response to equipment failures or breakdowns.
Advantages of corrective maintenance:-

  • Immediate response to urgent issues.
  • May be cost-effective for less critical equipment.
Considerations
Can result in unplanned downtime and higher repair costs.

Reliability-centered maintenance(RCM)

Analytical approach to identify the most efficient maintenance strategies for critical assets.
Advantages of RCM:-

  • Optimizes maintenance efforts based on criticality.
  • Aligns maintenance activities with business goals
Considerations
Requires a detailed analysis of asset criticality.

Condition-based maintenance

Maintenance is performed based on the actual condition of equipment, as determined by monitoring parameters such as vibration, temperature, or fluid levels.
Advantages of condition-based maintenance:-

  • Minimizes unnecessary maintenance.
  • Reduces the risk of over-maintenance.
Considerations
Requires reliable condition monitoring systems.

Total productive maintenance(TPM)

Focuses on maximizing the productivity of equipment by involving all employees in the maintenance process.
Advantages of TPM:-

  • Enhances overall equipment effectiveness.
  • Promotes a culture of continuous improvement.
Considerations
Requires training and engagement of all personnel.

Run-to-failure(RTF) maintenance

Allows equipment to run until it fails, then addresses issues as needed.
Advantages of RTF:-

  • Simple approach for non-critical equipment.
  • Minimizes proactive maintenance costs.
Considerations
Can lead to higher repair costs and downtime.

Proactive maintenance

Identifies and addresses potential issues before they cause equipment failure.
Advantages of proactive maintenance:-

  • Minimizes the risk of major breakdowns.
  • Improves overall equipment reliability.
Considerations
Requires thorough analysis and planning.

Outsourced maintenance

Engaging external service providers to perform maintenance activities.
Advantages of outsourced maintenance:-

  • Access to specialized expertise.
  • Cost-effective for certain tasks.
Considerations
Requires effective management of vendor relationships.

Documenting procedure for approval

Documenting the procedure for the approval of a maintenance strategy is crucial to ensure clarity, transparency and a systematic approach to gaining the necessary approvals.

Maintenance strategy approval procedure

The purpose of this document is to outline the step-by-step procedure for the approval of the maintenance strategy within organization.

Step to maintenance strategy approval procedure:-

  • Initiation of maintenance strategy development

    Activity perform in initiation of maintenance strategy development:-

    • Identify the need for the development or update of the maintenance strategy based on organizational goals, changes in equipment or other relevant factors.
    • Assemble a cross-functional team with representatives from maintenance, operations, safety and other relevant departments.

  • Drafting the maintenance strategy

    Activity perform in drafting the maintenance strategy:-

    • Develop a comprehensive maintenance strategy document that includes details on preventive, predictive and corrective maintenance approaches, key performance indicators (KPIs) and risk assessments.
    • Ensure alignment with organizational objectives and industry best practices.

  • Internal review

    Activity perform in internal review:-

    • Conduct an internal review of the draft maintenance strategy document.
    • Seek input and feedback from key stakeholders, including maintenance personnel,operations, safety officers and relevant managers.

  • Revision and improvement

    Activity perform in revision and improvement:-

    • Incorporate feedback from the internal review into the maintenance strategy document.
    • Make necessary revisions to enhance clarity, completeness and effectiveness.

  • Management approval request

    Activity perform in management approval request:-

    • Prepare a formal request for approval, including the revised maintenance strategy document and a summary of changes made based on internal feedback.
    • Submit the approval request to the management team for review.

  • Management review

    Activity perform in management review:-

    • Review the submitted maintenance strategy document and the associated approval request.
    • Evaluate the alignment of the strategy with organizational goals, regulatory requirements and industry standards.
    • Provide feedback and request additional information or revisions if necessary.

  • Revision and finalization

    Activity perform in revision and finalization:-

    • Address any feedback or requests for revisions from the management team.
    • Make final adjustments to the maintenance strategy document to ensure it meets all requirements and expectations.

  • Approval submission

    Activity perform in approval submission:-

    • Submit the finalized maintenance strategy document, along with any supporting documentation, to the designated approver for formal approval.

  • Formal approval

    Activity perform in formal approval:-

    • Review the submitted maintenance strategy document.
    • Approve or reject the document based on alignment with organizational goals, feasibility and compliance with standards.
    • If approved, provide a formal written approval.

  • Implementation and monitoring

    Activity perform in implementation and monitoring;-

    • Implement the approved maintenance strategy as outlined in the document.
    • Monitor and measure key performance indicators to ensure the strategy's effectiveness.

  • Periodic review and update

    Activity perform in periodic review and update:-

    • Schedule periodic reviews of the maintenance strategy to assess its effectiveness.
    • Update the strategy as needed based on Theorys learned, changes in equipment or shifts in organizational priorities.

Unit 4: Client and supplier processes and standards

Creating reporting procedures for service requests

Creating effective reporting procedures for service requests is essential to ensure that requests are properly documented, tracked and addressed in a timely manner.

Service request reporting procedures

The purpose of these reporting procedures is to establish a structured and efficient process for capturing, documenting and addressing service requests within organization.

Some service request reporting procedures:-

  • Request submission Ensure a standardized method for submitting service requests.

    Activity perform in request submission:-

    • Clients or users should submit service requests through email, phone and etc...
    • Include mandatory fields such as contact information, description of the issue and priority level.

  • Request categorization Classify service requests based on predefined categories.

    Activity perform in request categorization:-

    • Upon submission, categorize the service request into relevant groups(IT, facilities, maintenance).
    • Use a standardized classification system for consistency.

  • Priority assignment Determine the urgency and impact of the service request.

    Activity perform in priority assignment:-

    • Assign a priority level
    • Use predefined criteria to determine priority.

  • Initial triage Conduct an initial assessment to gather additional details.

    Activity perform in initial triage:-

    • Designate a triage team to review and gather additional information.
    • If necessary, communicate with the requester to clarify details.

  • Ticket creation Formally document the service request as a ticket.

    Activity perform in ticket creation:-

    • Create a dedicated ticket for each service request in the ticketing system.
    • Include all relevant details, categorization and priority level

  • Assignment to responsible party Assign the service request to the appropriate department or individual

    Activity perform in assignment to responsible party:-

    • Assign the ticket to the relevant department or individual responsible for addressing the request.
    • Clearly communicate responsibilities and timelines.

  • Acknowledgment to requester Confirm receipt of the service request to the requester.

    Activity perform in acknowledgment to requester:-

    • Send an acknowledgment to the requester, confirming that the service request has been received.
    • Provide a unique ticket reference for future correspondence.

  • Resolution time frame agreement Agree on a time frame for issue resolution.

    Activity perform in resolution time frame agreement:-

    • Work with the responsible party to determine a realistic resolution time frame.
    • Communicate the expected resolution time to the requester.

  • Regular updates to requester Keep the requester informed about the status of their service request.

    Activity perform in regular updates to requester:-

    • Provide regular updates on the progress of the request.
    • Communicate any delays or changes in the resolution time frame.

Creating escalation procedures

Creating effective escalation procedures based on a maintenance strategy is crucial to ensure that issues are addressed promptly and efficiently.

Maintenance strategy escalation procedures

The purpose of these escalation procedures is to establish a clear and structured process for escalating issues within the maintenance strategy framework in organization.

Escalation levels:-

  • Initial triage and response
    • Responsibility for maintenance team
    • Objective: Address and resolve routine maintenance issues.
    • Activity perform: Maintenance team conducts initial triage and resolves issues within standard response times | Regularly communicate progress to the requester.
  • Departmental escalation
    • Responsibility for department head or supervisor
    • Objective: Resolve issues that require higher expertise or resources.
    • Activity perform: Escalate the issue to the relevant department head or supervisor if resolution is beyond Level 1. | Provide additional resources or expertise as needed.
  • Management review:-
    • Responsibility for maintenance manager or equivalent.
    • Objective: Address escalated issues that cannot be resolved at lower levels.
    • Activity perform: Conduct a comprehensive review of the escalated issue. | Involve higher management if necessary for decision-making or resource allocation.
  • Executive escalation
    • Responsibility for chief operations officer(COO) or equivalent.
    • Objective: Resolve critical issues that impact operations or business continuity.
    • Activity perform: Escalate the issue to the COO or equivalent executive for immediate attention. | Mobilize additional resources or make strategic decisions.

Escalation triggers:-

  • Issue severity:

    • Trigger: The severity level of the maintenance issue exceeds predefined thresholds.
    • Actions: Automatically escalate issues with high severity to the next level.

  • Resolution time exceeded:

    • Trigger: The maintenance issue has not been resolved within the agreed-upon time frame.
    • Actions: If the resolution time exceeds predefined limits, escalate the issue to the next level.

  • Recurring issues:

    • Trigger: The same issue recurs within a short time frame.
    • Actions: Escalate issues that exhibit a pattern of recurrence for in-depth analysis and preventive measures.

  • Client or stakeholder escalation request:

    • Trigger: The client or a stakeholder explicitly requests escalation due to dissatisfaction or urgency.
    • Actions: Respond promptly to client or stakeholder requests for escalation and initiate the appropriate level.

Escalation process:-

  • Identification

    Action perform in identification:-

    • Clearly define criteria for identifying issues that require escalation.
    • Establish communication channels for stakeholders to request escalation.

  • Notification

    Action perform in notification:-

    • Implement an automated notification system to alert relevant parties about the escalation.
    • Ensure timely communication to all stakeholders involved.

  • Documentation

    Action perform in documentation:-

    • Document all details of the escalated issue, including history, actions taken and responses.
    • Maintain a central repository for easy access and reference.

  • Analysis and review

    Action perform in analysis and review:-

    • Conduct a thorough analysis of the escalated issue.
    • Review previous escalations for patterns and trends.

  • Decision making

    Action perform in decision making:-

    • Establish a clear decision-making process for each escalation level.
    • Define the authority and responsibilities of each level.

  • Resolution and follow-up

    Action perform in resolution and follow-up:-

    • Work towards a resolution of the escalated issue.
    • Follow up with stakeholders and document the resolution process.

  • Feedback and improvement

    Action perform in feedback and improvement:-

    • Collect feedback from stakeholders involved in the escalation process.
    • Use insights to continually improve the maintenance strategy and escalation procedures.

Setting help desk or other support function

Setting up a help desk or support function based on a maintenance strategy involves establishing a structured system to receive, prioritize and resolve maintenance-related issues efficiently.

Guide to help you design and implement a help desk or support function aligned with your maintenance strategy:-

  • Define objectives

    Clearly articulate the objectives of the help desk within the context of the maintenance strategy.
    Determine the primary goals, such as minimizing downtime, optimizing asset performance and ensuring client satisfaction.

  • Choose a support model

    Decide on a support model that aligns with your maintenance strategy.
    Common models include:-
    Centralized help desk: A single point of contact for all maintenance-related issues.
    Decentralized support teams: Separate teams for different maintenance areas.
    Self-service portals: Enable users to troubleshoot and resolve common issues independently.

  • Staffing and skills

    Identify the skills and expertise required for support staff.
    Determine the staffing levels needed based on the volume and complexity of maintenance issues.

  • Ticketing system

    Implement a robust ticketing system to track and manage maintenance requests.
    Ensure that the system allows for categorization, prioritization, and easy assignment of tickets.

  • Communication channels

    Define multiple channels for reporting maintenance issues.
    Clearly communicate these channels to all stakeholders.

  • Response time standards

    Establish response time standards for acknowledging and addressing maintenance requests.
    Differentiate between response times based on issue severity and impact on operations.

  • Categorization and prioritization

    Develop a standardized system for categorizing and prioritizing maintenance issues.
    Align categorization with the maintenance strategy key components.

  • Escalation procedures

    Create escalation procedures for issues that cannot be resolved at the initial support level.
    Define triggers for escalation, such as severity, resolution time or client request.

  • Knowledge base

    Establish a comprehensive knowledge base with troubleshooting guides, FAQs, and solutions.
    Encourage support staff to continuously update and expand the knowledge base

  • Training programs

    Provide ongoing training for support staff to enhance technical skills and improve issue resolution capabilities.
    Include training on the organization's maintenance strategy and processes.

  • Client communication

    Implement a communication plan to keep clients informed about the status of their maintenance requests.
    Provide regular updates, especially for issues with longer resolution times.

  • Performance metrics

    Define key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure the effectiveness of the help desk.
    Include metrics such as response time, resolution time, client satisfaction, and ticket closure rates

  • Continuous improvement

    Establish a process for continuous improvement based on feedback, data analysis, and industry best practices.
    Regularly review and update support processes to enhance efficiency.

  • Integration with maintenance software

    Integrate the help desk system with other maintenance software tools.
    Ensure seamless information flow between support functions and maintenance activities.

  • Client education and awareness

    Educate clients on how to effectively use the help desk and report maintenance issues.
    Promote awareness of preventive maintenance measures to reduce support requests.

  • Documentation and reporting

    Maintain detailed documentation of all maintenance requests, resolutions and feedback.
    Generate regular reports to analyze trends, identify recurring issues and measure overall performance.

  • Client feedback mechanism

    Implement a system for collecting client feedback on support experiences.
    Use feedback to identify areas for improvement and acknowledge successful resolutions.

  • Collaboration with maintenance teams

    Foster collaboration between the help desk and maintenance teams.
    Establish clear communication channels for transferring escalated issues or seeking expert advice.

  • Security and compliance

    Ensure that the help desk complies with security and privacy standards, especially when dealing with sensitive maintenance information.

  • Emergency response plan

    Develop a clear plan for handling emergency maintenance situations.
    Define roles, responsibilities and communication procedures during emergencies.

  • Regular audits and assessments

    Conduct regular audits and assessments of the help desk's performance.
    Use audits to identify areas for improvement and ensure compliance with established standards.

  • Documentation repository

    Create a centralized repository for all help desk documentation, including procedures, training materials, and policies.
